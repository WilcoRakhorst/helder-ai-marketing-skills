# Sources — SEO Keyword Research Skill

This document provides evidence for the claims made in this skill, backed by authoritative industry sources.

---

## 1. Title tags: 50-60 characters

**Claim:** Title tags should be 50-60 characters long.

**Evidence:**
- Moz: "Titles between 51–60 characters tend to result in the fewest rewrites by Google." — [Moz – What Are Title Tags?](https://moz.com/learn/seo/title-tag) (updated March 2025)
- Moz: "Moz recommends keeping your titles under 60 characters."
- Moz study (Dr. Peter J. Meyers, 57,832 title tags): Google rewrote 58% of title tags; titles of 51-60 characters were rewritten least often.
- Google Search Central: "There's no limit on how long a `<title>` element can be, the title link is truncated in Google Search results as needed." — [Google – Influencing title links](https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/title-link) (updated December 2025)

---

## 2. Four types of search intent

**Claim:** Search intent falls into Transactional, Commercial Investigation, Informational, and Navigational.

**Evidence:**
- Ahrefs: "SEOs usually group keywords into one of four keyword search intent buckets: Informational intent, Commercial intent, Transactional intent, Navigational intent." — [Ahrefs – Search Intent in SEO](https://ahrefs.com/blog/search-intent/) (updated October 2024)
- Backlinko/Semrush: "99% of all search terms fall under 4 different intent categories: informational, navigational, commercial and transactional." — [Backlinko – Search Intent](https://backlinko.com/hub/seo/search-intent) (updated February 2026)
- Google Quality Rater Guidelines confirm multiple intent types per search query.

---

## 3. Featured Snippets: 40-60 word definitions

**Claim:** Featured Snippet paragraph definitions are optimally 40-60 words.

**Evidence:**
- Semrush study: "The average definition Featured Snippet is between 40-60 words long." — Cited by [Backlinko – Featured Snippets](https://backlinko.com/hub/seo/featured-snippets) (updated October 2025), original source: [Semrush – How to Rank for Featured Snippets (2018)](https://www.semrush.com/blog/large-scale-study-how-to-rank-for-featured-snippets-in-2018/)
- Backlinko: "You need to provide Google a short (40-60 word) snippet of text that they can directly use in the Featured Snippet."
- Moz: "Google favors answers that are provided clearly and concisely within one paragraph." — [Moz – How to Optimize for Featured Snippets](https://moz.com/blog/optimize-featured-snippets) (updated August 2024)

---

## 4. Keyword Difficulty: backlinks as primary factor

**Claim:** Keyword difficulty is primarily determined by the number and quality of backlinks to top-ranking pages.

**Evidence:**
- Ahrefs: "We pull the top 10 ranking pages for your keyword and look up how many websites link to each of them. The more links the top-ranking pages for your keyword have, the higher its KD score." — [Ahrefs – Keyword Difficulty](https://ahrefs.com/blog/keyword-difficulty/) (updated December 2025)
- Ahrefs: "Google still relies on link-based signals like PageRank to rank search results, so knowing the backlink strength of top-ranking pages is still one of the best predictors of how hard a keyword is to win."
- Mark Williams-Cook (LinkedIn): Google still uses a modified version of PageRank.

---

## 5. Forums/Reddit in top 10 = low difficulty

**Claim:** If forums or Reddit posts rank in the top 10, the keyword is easier to rank for.

**Evidence:**
- Ahrefs: "Are there forums or low-authority blogs ranking among the giants? If a low-authority site has managed to rank, it's a strong signal that the 'giants' aren't satisfying the intent perfectly." — [Ahrefs – Keyword Difficulty](https://ahrefs.com/blog/keyword-difficulty/)
- Omid Ghiam (Marketer Milk): Uses DR filtering in Ahrefs to find keywords where low-DR sites rank (e.g. DR <15), as an indicator of low competition.

---

## 6. Keyword cannibalization: one primary keyword per page

**Claim:** One primary keyword per page to avoid cannibalization.

**Evidence:**
- Ahrefs: "Keyword cannibalization is an SEO issue that happens when multiple pages on a website target the same or similar keywords. This leads to a situation where the site's pages compete against each other for search engine rankings." — [Ahrefs – Keyword Cannibalization](https://ahrefs.com/blog/keyword-cannibalization/) (updated January 2024)
- Ahrefs case study: After consolidating two cannibalizing pages about "broken link building," both rankings and traffic increased significantly.

---

## 7. SERP Features: Featured Snippet, Local Pack, People Also Ask

**Claim:** SERP features influence keyword strategy; structured content (lists, tables) increases the chance of earning Featured Snippets.

**Evidence:**
- Moz: Featured snippets have an average CTR of 35%. — [Engine Scout study](https://enginescout.com.au/featured-snippets-study/), cited by [Moz](https://moz.com/blog/optimize-featured-snippets)
- Backlinko: "A Featured Snippet gets approximately 8% of all clicks." — [Search Engine Land study](https://searchengineland.com/another-featured-snippet-study-shows-steal-significant-traffic-first-organic-result-275967)
- Semrush: 4.77% of all queries display a Featured Snippet. — [Semrush Sensor](https://www.semrush.com/sensor/)
- Ahrefs: "99% of all Featured Snippets are from pages that already ranked on page 1."
- Moz: Use H2/H3 subheadings, bullet points, and tables to optimize for snippets.

---

## 8. Content Gap Analysis

**Claim:** Analyze topics competitors don't cover, questions they don't answer (People Also Ask), and thin/outdated content.

**Evidence:**
- Ahrefs: Content Gap tool compares keyword rankings between pages to find missing subtopics. — [Ahrefs – Search Intent](https://ahrefs.com/blog/search-intent/)
- Backlinko: "People also ask… boxes are a GOLDMINE of User Intent info. These boxes are literally telling you: 'These are questions people have around this topic.'" — [Backlinko – Search Intent](https://backlinko.com/hub/seo/search-intent)
- Moz: "Research popular questions" via People Also Ask, Reddit, and Quora. — [Moz – Featured Snippets](https://moz.com/blog/optimize-featured-snippets)

---

## 9. Search intent determines content type

**Claim:** Content type (blog, service page, tool) must match the dominant search intent in the SERP.

**Evidence:**
- Ahrefs case study: Changed landing page to free tool → rose from #8 to #1 for "backlink checker". — [Ahrefs – Keyword Difficulty](https://ahrefs.com/blog/keyword-difficulty/)
- Ahrefs: "We got 516% more traffic in less than six months by making a change to one of our landing pages" by matching search intent. — [Ahrefs – Search Intent](https://ahrefs.com/blog/search-intent/)
- Backlinko: Rewrote "SEO strategy" post from case study to high-level guide → now ranks as Featured Snippet. — [Backlinko – Search Intent](https://backlinko.com/hub/seo/search-intent)

---

## Note on recency

8 out of 9 sources were updated in 2024-2026. The only older source is the Semrush Featured Snippets study from 2018 (regarding the 40-60 word length). However, this claim is still actively cited by Backlinko (October 2025) and used as an industry-standard guideline — no newer study has contradicted it.
