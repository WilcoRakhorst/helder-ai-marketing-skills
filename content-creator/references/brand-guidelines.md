# Brand Voice & Style Guidelines

## Brand voice framework

### 1. Voice dimensions

#### Formality spectrum
- **Formal**: Legal documents, investor communication, crisis communication
- **Professional**: B2B content, whitepapers, case studies
- **Conversational**: Blog posts, social media, email newsletters
- **Informal**: Community, behind-the-scenes content

#### Tone attributes
Choose 3-5 primary attributes for your brand:
- **Authoritative**: Position as industry expert
- **Friendly**: Approachable and warm
- **Innovative**: Forward-thinking and creative
- **Trustworthy**: Solid and transparent
- **Inspiring**: Motivating and uplifting
- **Educational**: Informative and helpful
- **Witty**: Clever and entertaining (use sparingly)

#### Perspective
- **First person plural (we/us)**: Creates feeling of collaboration
- **Second person (you)**: Direct and engaging
- **Third person**: Objective and professional

### 2. Brand personality archetypes

Choose one primary and one secondary archetype:

**The Expert**
- Tone: Knowledgeable, confident, informative
- Content: Data-driven, well-supported, educational
- Example: "Our research shows that 87% of businesses..."

**The Friend**
- Tone: Warm, supportive, conversational
- Content: Relatable, helpful, encouraging
- Example: "We get it — marketing can be overwhelming..."

**The Innovator**
- Tone: Visionary, bold, forward-thinking
- Content: Innovative, disruptive, trend-setting
- Example: "The future of marketing is here..."

**The Guide**
- Tone: Wise, patient, instructional
- Content: Step-by-step, clear, concrete
- Example: "Let's walk through this together..."

**The Motivator**
- Tone: Energetic, positive, inspiring
- Content: Empowering, action-oriented, transformative
- Example: "You have the power to transform your business..."

### 3. Writing principles

#### Clarity first
- Use simple words where possible
- Break complex ideas into digestible pieces
- Lead with the main message
- Use active voice (80% of the time)

#### Reader-focused
- Focus on benefits, not features
- Address pain points directly
- Use "you" more often than "we"
- Include success stories

#### Consistency
- Keep the voice the same across all channels
- Use approved terminology
- Follow formatting standards
- Apply style rules uniformly

### 4. Language guidelines

#### Words to use
- **Action verbs**: Transform, accelerate, optimize, unlock, empower
- **Positive descriptors**: Powerful, intuitive, strategic
- **Results-oriented**: Results, growth, success, impact, ROI

#### Words to avoid
- **Jargon**: Synergy, leverage (as a verb), bandwidth (for availability)
- **Overused**: Innovative, disruptive, groundbreaking (unless truly applicable)
- **Weak**: Very, really, just, maybe, hopefully
- **Negative**: Can't, won't, impossible, problem (use "challenge")

### 5. Content structure templates

#### Blog post structure
```
Hook (attention-grabbing opening)
  → Bridge (connect to reader's situation)
    → Body (main content, structured with H2/H3)
      → Conclusion (summary + key takeaway)
        → CTA (clear next step)
```

#### Email structure
```
Subject line (curiosity or benefit)
  → Personal opening (1-2 sentences)
    → Value (main content, scannable)
      → Single CTA (one clear action)
        → P.S. (optional, reinforces CTA)
```
